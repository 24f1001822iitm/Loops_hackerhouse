IV. If you have a policy

You pay to be covered. Not to be on hold.

Something breaks at the wrong hour — a cracked windshield on the way to work, a ceiling that starts leaking right as the guests arrive, a fender that crumples in a parking lot at 11 p.m. You reach for the phone and remember: the number on the back of the card is only staffed during business hours, and what answers first is a menu of seven options, none of which is yours.

“It’s 2 a.m. Your basement is flooding. Alex picks up on the second ring.”

Alex changes the shape of that call. She’s available at any hour, already knows your policy by the time you’ve said your name, and can file the claim by voice while you’re still standing in the water. She texts you a reference number before you hang up, and she hands the line to a human the moment you ask — or the moment the situation actually needs one.

Alex only sees what your insurer lets her see. Every tool call hits your insurer’s own backend. No conversation data leaves their system unless you ask it to.

What Alex will actually do for you

  • File a new claim By voice, in under three minutes.
  • Check status At any hour, in any tone of voice.
  • List what’s missing So you know which document is blocking the adjuster.
  • Schedule a human call If you’d rather not explain it to a machine.
  • Request a supervisor And get a reference number before you hang up.

The line that used to cost you an afternoon.
