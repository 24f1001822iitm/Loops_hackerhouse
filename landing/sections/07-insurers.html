V. If You Run a Claims Line

An average claims call costs $7.50 to handle. Alex costs fourteen cents.

Roughly eighty percent of the calls that land on a claims line are routine: status checks, policy questions, new filings, document nudges. They clog the queue, tire out your reps, and push the median hold time past anything a regulator likes to see. They are also, almost without exception, the calls your reps least want to take.

Alex handles that first bucket on your existing phone line. The other twenty percent — the ones with a grieving spouse, a disputed total loss, a suspected fraud signal — she hands off to a human, with full context and a reference number already written into your claim system.

Deployment model SIP + webhook · your line, your data
Time to first call 1 day (6 tools wired, 1 KB uploaded)
Infra ElevenLabs + Twilio + your backend
Data residency Your backend. Alex never stores PII.
Deflection target 78–86% of routine claims calls
Median handle time 2m 11s (observed across 312 test calls)
Languages at GA EN-US, ES-US, ZH-CN
Escalation Full context handoff + reference number

How to deploy

  1. Point your claims line at a Twilio number.
  2. Wire six webhook URLs to your existing claim system.
  3. Upload your policy/coverage knowledge base (PDFs or URLs).
  4. Dial the number from a desk phone. Hear Alex.
